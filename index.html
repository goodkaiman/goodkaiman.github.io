Condensed Matter Research

Under the supervision of Valentin Taufour in the UC Davis Physics Department, I am on a team of researchers that synthesizes and characterizes exotic properties of new materials. I grew this beautiful purple crystal through anodic electrodeposition and detailed process refinement in order to increase sample purity and improve superconductive response. This has given me the amazing opportunity to independently manage and develop my own scientific project. Learn more about the research team here!


Origami Radiation Shield

I love origami and its seemingly limitless applications in engineering. I came up with this design as a submission for a crowdsourced NASA competition looking for conceptual origami radiation shields for future Mars mission habitats. The entries were judged on their ability to pack efficiently, deploy easily, and their level of protection. The design must fit inside a fairing of given dimention, encapsulate a pod with as many layers of material as possible, and be easily deployable. I finished among the 75th percentile and with an even deeper passion for origami. Feel free to check out my actual submission and some of my other origami work.
